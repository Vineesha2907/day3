chapter2

Set in the American South during the 1930s, it addresses issues of racial injustice and moral growth. The story is narrated by Scout Finch, a young girl, and follows her experiences in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama. Atticus Finch, Scout's father, is an attorney defending Tom Robinson, a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman, Mayella Ewell. The novel explores racial prejudice, social inequality, and the loss of innocence. Despite Atticus's compelling defense, the jury convicts Robinson due to the prevailing racial bias. The title "To Kill a Mockingbird" symbolizes the innocence and harmlessness of certain individuals. Atticus explains to Scout and her brother Jem that it is a sin to kill a mockingbird because they only bring beauty through their songs and do no harm.
