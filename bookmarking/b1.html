"The Hunger Games" is a dystopian novel written by Suzanne Collins. Published in 2008, the book is set in a future post-apocalyptic world where the government, known as the Capitol, exercises control over twelve districts. The story revolves around Katniss Everdeen, a young girl from District 12, who volunteers to participate in the Hunger Games—an annual televised event where one boy and one girl from each district must fight to the death until only one survives. The novel explores themes of survival, government oppression, and the consequences of violence. It gained widespread popularity for its compelling narrative and strong, resourceful female protagonist. "The Hunger Games" is the first book in a trilogy, followed by "Catching Fire" and "Mockingjay." Hunger Games" follows the story of Katniss Everdeen, a 16-year-old girl living in the dystopian nation of Panem. Panem is divided into the Capitol and twelve districts, with the Capitol exerting control over the districts through various means, including the Hunger Games. The Hunger Games are an annual event where one boy and one girl, known as "tributes," are selected from each district to participate in a televised fight to the death. The purpose is to remind the districts of the Capitol's power and control. When Katniss's younger sister, Prim, is chosen as the female tribute from District 12, Katniss volunteers to take her place.
